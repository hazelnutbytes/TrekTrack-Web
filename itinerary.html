Overview

The Kedarkantha Trek is one of India’s most popular winter treks, known for its snow-covered trails and breathtaking Himalayan views. Located in Uttarakhand’s Govind Wildlife Sanctuary, it is considered an ideal trek for beginners as well as adventure enthusiasts.

The trek starts from the village of Sankri and passes through beautiful forests of pine and oak before reaching the Kedarkantha summit at around 12,500 feet. From the top, trekkers are rewarded with stunning panoramic views of the surrounding Himalayan peaks.

Day-by-Day Itinerary

Day 1 – Arrival at Sankri

Arrive at the quaint village of Sankri and check into the guesthouse. Meet your trek leader and fellow trekkers for a briefing session covering safety, route details, and trekking tips. Spend the evening exploring the village, taking photos of the surrounding pine forests, and enjoying the serene sunset. Overnight stay at the guesthouse with light dinner and rest.

Day 2 – Sankri to Juda Ka Talab

Begin a 4–5 km trek through dense pine and oak forests with gradual ascents. Along the trail, enjoy panoramic views of snow-capped peaks and glacial streams. Stop for tea breaks at scenic spots and learn basic trekking techniques. Reach the Juda Ka Talab campsite by afternoon, set up tents, and participate in a team briefing on campsite etiquette. Evening campfire and overnight stay in tents.

Day 3 – Juda Ka Talab to Base Camp

Continue the ascent toward Kedarkantha Base Camp. The trail includes gentle snow patches (in winter), small river crossings, and breathtaking Himalayan views. Pause for photography and short rests to enjoy the flora and fauna. Reach the Base Camp by late afternoon, set up tents, and enjoy hot beverages with fellow trekkers. Evening includes preparation for the summit climb and overnight stay in tents.

Day 4 – Summit Day

Start very early (around 4 AM) for the summit climb to 12,500 ft. Trek under the starry sky before sunrise, crossing snow-laden paths and ridge trails. Reach the summit to witness breathtaking sunrise views over the Himalayas. Spend some time at the top capturing photos and enjoying the scenery. Descend carefully back to the Base Camp by afternoon. Evening includes a celebratory campfire and dinner at camp. Overnight stay in tents.

Day 5 – Base Camp to Sankri

Begin your descent through forested trails, retracing your route to Sankri. Stop along the way for short breaks and final photographs of the scenic landscapes. Reach Sankri by afternoon, check into the guesthouse, freshen up, and participate in a small trek completion ceremony. Evening at leisure to interact with fellow trekkers or explore the village market. Overnight stay at guesthouse.

Day 6 – Departure

Enjoy breakfast at Sankri and bid farewell to the trekking team. Depart for Dehradun with wonderful memories and a collection of photos and experiences. Optional stops at local points of interest en route for souvenirs or scenic photography. End of the Kedarkantha trek adventure.

Frequently Asked Questions

🏔 What kind of terrain will we encounter?
The Kedarkantha trek passes through gentle forest trails, pine and oak forests, snow patches in winter, and ridge trails with panoramic Himalayan views.
📝 Are permits required?
Yes, forest permits are mandatory. Trek leaders usually handle the formalities.
🫁 Is oxygen available for high altitude?
Trek leaders carry first aid kits and emergency oxygen cylinders.
🎒 What should I pack?
Essentials include trekking shoes, warm clothing, gloves, hats, water bottles, sunscreen, and sunglasses.
❄ When is the best time to do the Kedarkantha Trek?
The best time is from December to April for snow trekking, and October to November for clear mountain views and pleasant weather.
💪 Is the trek suitable for beginners?
Yes, Kedarkantha is considered a beginner-friendly Himalayan trek with gradual climbs and well-defined trails.
